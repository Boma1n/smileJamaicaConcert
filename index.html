Story

The Smile Jamaica concert is a historic concert held on December 5, 1976, at the National Heroes Park in Kingston, Jamaica, in which Bob Marley & the Wailers participated.

Two days earlier, the singer was shot at his home on Hope Road. He survived his injuries. In spite of this, Marley had wanted to do this concert: while he had planned to perform only one song, he performed for an hour and a half in front of 80,000 spectators. All the Wailers were present, except for bassist Aston "Familyman" Barrett, who was replaced by Cat Coore from Third World.

Bob Marley

The Wailers

A few weeks after the concert, Bob Marley left his native island for London where he stayed for 14 months. He only returned to Jamaica for the One Love Peace Concert in 1978.

More

Jamaican television has a morning show called Smile Jamaica, an obvious nod to the popularity of the concert series.

Stir It Up: The CIA Targets Jamaica, Bob Marley and the Progressive Manley Government, an alternative history novel by David Dusty Couples, blends fact and fiction to dramatize the concert.

Marlon James' novel A Brief History of Seven Killings dramatizes the attempt on Marley's life, including other events leading up to the Smile Jamaica concert.
